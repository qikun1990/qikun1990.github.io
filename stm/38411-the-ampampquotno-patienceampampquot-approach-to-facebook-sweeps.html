    The "No Patience" Approach To Facebook Sweeps

    Hey,

    So, I'm dipping my toes into the world of running affiliate offers on Facebook. I've chosen to go down the route of sweepstakes, and as I'm an eager beaver I went for the first offer I saw that I thought would do well in the US... yep I have no patience, it was the only offer I was approved on so I thought whilst I wait for other tier 2 & 3 offers I'm hitting this.

    Geo: US
    Offers: 2
    Payout: $2.30
    Ads: 3
    Lander: 1
    Tracker: CPVLab

    The plan...

    The plan is to hit $1000/day FAST! Ambitious... yes. Risky... yes. Doable... damn right! I've got a decent bit of experience and knowledge with running Facebook Ads (I run a FB ad agency), mainly local lead gen stuff but I do a lot of online as well. I know AM is gonna be a whole lot different to local lead gen but I've got the basics down.

    I'm going with PPE (Post Engagement) ads, for a few reasons...

    1) Through testing with my agency clients ads/money (one of the perks of having an agency), Link Click ads under perform 9/10 (my own opinion through my own testing). Yes, you get a cheaper click, but that's exactly who FB show your ad to... people most likely to click, not convert... like 69 year old Doris who has no idea what she's clicking on but just clicks for the sake of it. I just find PPE ads target a better quality section of audience.

    2) Following on from 1), I'm running one of those "Giveaway" offers. We all know the people who convert on these offers are the ones who engage, they always are told to like, share, comment to enter in different contests and freebies.

    3) Potentially get a nice chunk of organic traffic from people engaging with your ad (liking, commenting, sharing). This should also reduce the CPC, Facebook love engagement. Hoping the engagement stays positive on the most part for as long as possible.

    4) Get a decent Post Engager audience to retarget as well as Landing Page visitors (Again through my own experience and testing with other peoples money, Engager audiences slay)

    Of course, it's mostly all based off my own experience... which has very little to do with running Sweepstake offers.

    Before starting, I kindly asked my AM to whack my pixel on the offer conversion page. He said he has, but hasn't give me the URL for me to build a custom conversion off of so that's useless until I can get that. Other than that, I've searched for a way to place the pixel from within the hasoffers dashboard but no luck so far.

    Anyway, on with the show...

    ======

    DAY #1 - Oct 23rd
    Clicks: 83
    LP CTR: 87.95%
    Conversions: 10
    Spend: $20.98
    Revenue: $23
    Profit: +$2.02
    ROI: 9.62%

    YAY! First day, and straight in the profit zone. CPC was decent, and a ton of engagement, people love a contest so say you perhaps ask them to comment or share with their friends... well that's a winner, winner, chicken dinner!

    ======

    DAY #2 - Oct 24th
    Clicks: 304
    LP CTR: 82.57%
    Conversions: 29
    Spend: $41.40
    Revenue: $66.70
    Profit: +$25.30
    ROI: 61.09%

    The engagement is working an absolute treat! It's ridiculous. I've got a clear winning ad by a long way but going to keep the others alive for another couple hundred clicks. Also looks like I have an offer pulling ahead, BTW the offer conversion is at 9.54%. I like to let my ads run for at least 48 hours before I start messing with them.

    ======

    DAY #3 - Oct 25th
    Clicks: 910
    LP CTR: 81.43%
    Conversions: 98
    Spend: $99.10
    Revenue: $225.40
    Profit: +$126.30
    ROI: 127.45%

    $100 DAY! Well, actually $200 DAY!!! Averaged just under $0.11c a click today. This morning I added in 2 Lookalike audiences... 1) LAL 1% of my LP visitors and 2) LAL 1% of my Post Engagers... they both got off to flying starts. I cut the 2 losing ads and duped the winning ad across all my audiences and ad sets. The LP is a dead simple click through page, literally just "click the button to enter". I've built out Lookalikes on 2% and 3% ready to test and scale. Plan is, milk this little cow for all I can till I get shut down or the offer goes.

    ======

    DAY #4 - Oct 26th
    Clicks: 1,898
    LP CTR: 83.51%
    Conversions: 210
    Spend: $213.71
    Revenue: $483
    Profit: $269.29
    ROI: 126%

    *** My AM just reached out at 22:00pm GMT. Today has been insane, I'll update the stats at the end of the day but I've doubled the volume (I got aggressive). He's asked me to slow down so he can run some quality checks with the Advertiser. I'm guessing this is part and parcel of the AM game. I'm a brand new affiliate on the network so don't have any prior. That said, in the meantime I'm going to kick off some higher paying offers whilst I wait ***

    *** well that was quick, the advertiser got back to my AM and all is good, they want more... they're gonna get it! ***

    So, as you can see I was busy scaling today. I split Android vs IOS into their own Campaigns. I started them off on lifetime budgets and ran them to my Lookalike 3% audiences. They got off to absolute flyers.

    Android users are converting on the offer at 14% with an ROI of 188%! IOS converted at 12% on the offer with 158% ROI.

    ======

    DAY #5 - Oct 27th
    Clicks: 2,203
    LP CTR: 82.75%
    Conversions: 246
    Spend: $257.75
    Revenue: $565.80
    Profit: $308.05
    ROI: 119.51%

    Got over the halfway mark to $1k! And a nice chunk of profit for a $2.30 payout on a US offer. I asked my AM for a bump, he said he'd negotiate with the Advertiser so we shall see. In fairness though, I'd be happy milking this at 100% ROI whilst I can. Rolled out some new ads today, same copy but a multi-image page post, seen these do really well with lead gen so hoping they perform well here as well.

    ======

    DAY #6 - Oct 28th

    LP CTR:
    Conversions:

    Revenue:

    ROI:

    *** Another message from my AM today asking about lead quality, apparently yesterday the quality of lead was worse than previous days. I decided to slow up on the scaling process and pull back a little. Although I wanted this to be a fast step to $1k/day, I also want to make sure I get paid and don't lose the offer.

    My thoughts on the poor quality of lead? Well, I'm not really sure what they mean by poor quality, they're US based and fit any/all criteria, I'm assuming they aren't completing the numerous surveys etc that they redirect them to after they give their details. Anyway, I've paused my worse performing audiences and only have Android running for now. I'm going to see how todays leads look if any better. ***
