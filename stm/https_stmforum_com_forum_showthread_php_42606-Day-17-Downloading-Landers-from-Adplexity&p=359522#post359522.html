    Day 17: Downloading Landers from Adplexity

    In the previous lesson, you've decided on a vertical, and a language to target (this can be for one geo, or for multiple geos that speak the same language). And if sweeps is what you've decided to run, then you've already decided on a sweepstakes prize as well.

    Below, we'll download some landers from Adplexity.

    If you don't have Adplexity, then you'll need to do some browsing "in the wild" as I've mentioned in a previous lesson - basically setting your VPN to the target geo, then keep going to different sites and clicking around to trigger pop ads that are landing pages (vs. other pop ads that are offer pages - when affiliates are direct-linking; and some are not even run by affiliates), and download them either by right-clicking and selecting "Save as" or by using a software like HTTRACK.

    If you're looking for sweeps landers that are in a specific language and for a specific prize - it would take forever to come across the right ones. Plus you won't know which landers are the most popular - which Adplexity will tell you.

    For these reasons, I would HIGHLY recommend getting a subscription to Adplexity Mobile - I've included links to discount pages in the previous lesson.

    Enough talk - let's take some ACTION!


    **************************************************

    ACTION


    1)Log into Adplexity Mobile. Set some of the filters in the left menu:

    "Filter" - This is the date filter. Set it to "Last 7 days" to only see landers that were detected to be running during the last 7 days. (Note: If you feel that you're not seeing enough landers, you can expand to "Last 30 days" - landers don't get saturated this quickly.)

    "Days Running" - Set to 7 days to infinity.




    "Ad Type" - Click "Choose More" to choose more than one option, then checkmark "Popup" and "Redirects" ("Redirects" are a similar traffic type to pop, and the same/similar landers are used).

    "Device Type" - Leave at default (no restrictions).

    "Size" - Leave at default - no need to select banner size because we're not looking for banners.

    "Country" and "Language" filters - Use these to specify the geo(s) and language you're wanting to find landers for. In most cases, specifying one of the two filters would suffice, but you can also specify both filters.

    "Connection" - Leave at default (no restrictions).

    "Traffic source" - Leave at default (no restrictions).

    "Affiliate network" - Leave at default (no restrictions).

    "Tracking Tool" - Click on "Choose More" > click on "Select all" > uncheck "None".


    2)Next, set some of the filters at the top of the screen:

    Make sure "BY KEYWORD" is selected.

    Leave "Keyword" dropdown set to "on the landing page", and enter the prize of the sweepstakes offers you've chosen to run - e.g. "iphone xs" - in the empty field. Set the filter at the top right to "Received most traffic".




    3)Now we're ready to choose some landers. Basically pick ones that look different from each other as much as possible.

    Pick 10-20 to start. Just click on each one you want to pick and Adplexity will open it up in a new tab.

    In each new tab, go to the "Landing Pages" section at the top > "Offer & Tracking" column, and click on the "Show More" button for any entry (all should have the same lander).




    This will bring up a "Landing Page Details" window. Scroll to the bottom and click on "DOWNLOAD THIS LANDING PAGE".




    Do this until you have 10-20 different-looking landers.




    **************************************************

    EXPLANATION

    -Note that the approach we're using here, is to include a wide variety of landers in our testing, so that chances are we'll capture at least a couple of decent landers. Therefore, I haven't specified a lot of restrictions in the filtering options. The downside of that approach is that it would take more money to test more landers.

    The alternative approach would be to be very selective in your choice of landers - for example choose a couple of landers that you feel would stand the best chances of converting well, and just test those. The latter approach would be less expensive, but your chances of ending up with a well-converting lander would be smaller. The choice would be up to you to make when you have more experience.

    Note: Also I've advised for you to download 10-20 landers, not all those will make it to the actual testing. When it comes time for you to "fix up" the coding on the landers, you may find that some are more difficult to fix than others - and having more landers downloaded means you can just skip the difficult ones. If you're an experienced coder, by all means just download 5 (which is the number of landers we'll actually be testing).


    -We could have done a search for just "iphone" to get even more landers. Since iphone xs offers are relatively new at the time of writing, there aren't as many landers available, and searching for just "iphone" would include more results. We could download iphone x landers for example, and just change the lander text and images to customize them for iphone x.


    -We don't need to stick to iphone landers - landers that are used to promote other sweeps offers, such as other electronics (PS4, iPads, Samsung phones...) or gift cards, can be customized for other prizes as well. That will, however, require more coding experience, time, and effort.


    -Taking that one step further: An advanced tactic would be to browse landers in other verticals - e.g. gambling, antivirus, even adult - and customize them for use with sweeps offers.

    -Another popular tactic is to search for landers used in tier 1 geo campaigns, and used them in the lower-tier geos (it may be necessary to get translations). This is a good technique because tier 1 geos tend to be more competitive and has higher profits potential, and therefore advertisers tend to spend more time on innovating to come up with new lander designs and angles, as compared to when dealing with lower-tier geos.

    -For "Language", you can specify multiple languages if your target country speaks multiple languages. You could set up tracker rules to detect the language of the visitor's device/browser, and redirect them to landers in the respective language. This is a good way to increase ROI.

    -If, when you specify both "Country" and "Language", you're not seeing a lot of landers for your type of offers, try to just specify the language without filtering by country - that way you'll see landers for all countries that speak that language.

    The reason I suggested tier 3/4 geos is because they tend to have lower competition. Of course you can target any geo you like - just be aware that for the more expensive and bigger tier 1 geos, you may need to test a lot more offers and landers and cut a lot more traffic segments (e.g. placements) to reach profits. In other words you'd need to invest a lot more money on testing and optimization.

    But if you like to give a specific geo a try, go for it, even if it happens to be a big tier 1 geo. Just be sure to set a reasonable daily budget so you have time to analyze stats and blacklist stuff in time.

    As for lack of landers, you can always rip landers in another language and have the text translated. If you rewrite the text into really short sentences you can even use google translate. Google translate has become pretty good in recent months - it's especially accurate when translating short sentences. Also make sure everything is literal, as google translate doesn't handle figures of speech too well sometimes.

    Profuse apologies for having missed your post!

    Wow those are some really great questions!

    Regarding threshold for the number of hits - I don't think anyone would be able to answer that question. Basically the more "hits" or occurrences, the more times it's been seen by the tool, which means the more traffic volume it has received.

    Obviously, the more traffic a campaign has received, and the longer (more number of days) it has been receiving high levels of traffic, the more promising the campaign - because no affiliate in their right mind would keep sending lots of traffic over more than a few days to a hopeless campaign.

    Of course, there are a LOT of other factors that will determine the success/failure of a campaign, such as offer payout (the other affiliate may have gotten pay bumps), placement targeting (the other affiliate may have whittled down to the best placements), and other campaign optimization (dayparting for example).

    Nevertheless, a high number of "hits" over at least a few days is still a WAY better indication than doing random testing.

    One thing to keep in mind is that some of your competitors are also using Adplexity, and they too will aim for the high-profile campaigns (that have received the most traffic over longer duration).

    Which means that by ripping these campaigns, you can expect more competition and quicker saturation than if you were to pick campaigns that have received less "hits".

    This isn't necessarily a bad thing - because it would be hard to know HOW saturated the campaign is at the present, until you actually test it.

    These are some things I would suggest:

    1)By all means, test campaigns that have received the most traffic over at least a few days. Yes, competition will be higher and saturation will be reached sooner, but there may still be time to make money before saturation sets in.

    2)Instead of looking at each campaign individually, try to look for TRENDS. e.g. Out of the top 50 campaigns in terms of hits, which vertical(s) is predominant?

    3)Don't rip a campaign as-is. Take a promising-looking campaign, gather a few other offers and landers in the same vertical (from other Adplexity campaigns that have received a decent number of hits - again "decent" being subjective), and split-test.

    4)Don't just rip campaigns, but angles. e.g. Pick 3rd/4th tier geo and analyze the top 50 campaigns to see which vertical(s) is hot, then look at campaigns in the same vertical for tier 1 geos to see what types of angles are working, and test those on the tier 3/4 geos.

    5)In addition to browsing the "hottest" campaigns that have the most hits, try some of the "lukewarm" ones further down the page that have received fewer hits. Those are the ones that haven't made it onto the radar of too many other Adplexity users. The uncertainty will increase (they're less "proven") but the competition level and speed of saturation would be less. I've found more than a few great campaigns this way.

    also wondering if you'd recommend using the data under 'BY ADVERTISE' to see whether direct linking or campaigns which use a landing page are getting more hits/performing better?
    Do you mean searching for campaigns launched by the same advertiser? Yes - by tracking what they're running and the number of hits each campaign is getting, over time, can be a great way to see what's working for them and what isn't.


    While spying on competitors' campaigns, what are the metrics that indicate it's too saturated already or if it's too competitive or too difficult for newbies to crack?
    Regarding saturation, that would be hard to gauge I think - until you actually test a campaign.

    Competition level you can gauge by analyzing the top campaigns by traffic volume - if a lot of campaigns are in a particular vertical then competition is probably high. However, competition level may not be that relevant when it comes to deciding whether or not to run in that vertical, or even that same specific offer. The reason why so many people are running the offer may be because the offer/vertical is hot at the moment. And you won't know how close to saturation it is until you test.

    Too difficult for newbies to crack - I would say verticals/offers that have higher payouts. Don't even touch those until you have a pretty good blacklist. And if possible, run lower-payout offers in the same vertical first, to build a white/blacklist, then target the best placements with higher-payout offers.

    Hope that wasn't too confusing! Please feel free to continue the discussion.
