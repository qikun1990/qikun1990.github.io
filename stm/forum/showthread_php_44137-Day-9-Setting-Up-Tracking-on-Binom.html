    Day 9: Setting Up Tracking on Binom

    ***********************************

    ACTION

    Today we'll be setting up the Binom tracker.

    A tracker will detect and display all kinds of stats about your visitors to help you optimize your campaigns - mainly to either identify which audiences are converting badly and stop targeting them, or to identify audiences that are converting well and target them solely.

    It will also allow you to specify how and where you want to send your visitors (e.g. directly to the offer, or to a landing page first and THEN to the offer), and will let you split-test multiple offers and landing pages in rotation.

    What we're doing in this lesson, is add into Binom the one or more offers you got approved for, which you've recorded on the offer campaigns spreadsheet, and then create campaigns in Binom for those offers.

    Go log into Binom right now - let's get right to it!





    Step 0) Before setting up Binom campaigns for the offers, let's first add the 2 traffic sources.

    In Binom, go to "Traffic sources" > "+ Create" > "Load from template" > starting typing "PropellerAds" in the search box and then click on the "PropellerAds" entry when it appears, and click "Save".

    Then, still on the "Traffic sources" page, click "+ Create" again and repeat th last step for PopAds.

    Next, we add the offers and create Binom campaigns for them.





    1)Let's start with the Mobidea offer. In Binom, click on the "Aff. networks" tab at the top > "+ Create" > click "Load from catelog" > starting typing "Mobidea" in the search box and then click on the "Mobidea" entry when it appears, and click "Save".


    2)Still in Binom, click on "Settings" in the top-right corner, find the first link in the "Postback URL" section, and click "Copy" to copy it.




    3)Log into Mobidea. Put the cursor on the gear icon (Settings) at the top, then click on "Global postback", select "Custom", paste the Postback URL you've copied from Binom into the "Insert your domain" field.

    Don't save it yet! We need to make 2 changes to the Postback URL:

    a)Replace "cnv_id={network_token}" with "cnv_id={{EXTERNAL_ID}}".

    b)Replace "payout={payout}" with "payout={{MONEY}}".

    So the original postback vs. final postback url would look something like:

    https://yourdomain.com/click.php?cnv...ayout={payout}

    https://yourdomain.com/click.php?cnv_id={{EXTERNAL_ID}}&payout={{MONEY}}
    Lastly: Scroll down, answer the security question, and click "Save Changes". (Note: You can set up security questions by clicking into Mobidea's Account Settings - the little person icon in the upper-right corner, then clicking on "Change security question".)



    (Note: This is the ONLY TIME you'll need to insert the postback into Mobidea! The postback will work for all Mobidea offers you run in the future - until you switch to another tracker of course!)


    4)Go to the Offers Spreadsheet you compiled yesterday. Copy the first link in the "Original Offer Link" column and paste it into the "Final Offer Link" in the same row. The link should look like this:

    https://www.rotatemyurls.com/offer/11111|13456?data1=Track1&data2=Track2&tag={Externa l_ID_from_traffic_source}&website={subID}&placemen t={sub_subID}
    Now for this "Final Offer Link", replace this portion:

    data1=Track1&data2=Track2&tag={External_ID_from_tr affic_source}&website={subID}&placement={sub_subID }
    With:

    tag={clickid}&website={trafficsource}&placement={c ampaign}
    So that the final offer link would look like this:

    https://www.rotatemyurls.com/offer/11111|13456?tag={clickid}&website={trafficsource}& placement={campaign}
    (IMPORTANT: Please make sure there are NO SPACES in the link! vBulletin has a glitch - it displays spaces that aren't there.)

    Copy this final offer link.


    5)Go back to Binom, click on "Offers" > "+ Create". Paste the final offer link you've just copied, into the "URL" field.


    6)Now we fill in the rest of the offer information in Binom, by copying from the Offers Spreadsheet.

    Copy the "Offer ID and Name" name from the spreadsheet, and paste into the "Name" field in Binom.

    For "Group" just leave blank for now (you can use this to group offers later, and only if you wish to).

    For "Country" select the target country of your offer.

    For "Aff. Network" select "Mobidea".

    For "Payout" checkmark "Auto" (for binom to display revenues based on payout amounts sent back by the aff network postback).

    For "Conversion Cap" leave unchecked for now (you can find out more about this feature if/when in the future, you run an offer that has caps).

    Hit "Save".


    7)Still in Binom, create new campaign:

    -Click on "Campaigns" > "+ Create".

    -Assign a campaign name. It's important to name your campaign properly so you don't mix them up, as after 10-20 campaigns you may not remember which is which. You'll develop your own campaign-naming convention later no doubt. For now, just name it in the format:

    [Date] [2-Letter Country Code] Main [Carrier] [Any Other Info You Want]

    For [Date] put the date you're starting the campaign on. You can use any format. I like to put [mmddyy], so if today is November 2nd 2018 (which it is as I'm writing this!) then I'd put "110218".

    [2-Letter Country Code] you can look up from here:

    http://www.worldatlas.com/aatlas/ctycodes.htm

    The "Main" stands for "Mainstream", which means non-adult.

    [Carrier] is of course the mobile carrier accepted by the offer, which you'll be targeting at the traffic source later. (If the offer accepts multiple carriers, just pick one to test for now.)

    As for [Any Other Info You Want], it's self-explanatory - basically anything you want. For example the offer name if you're running one offer. Or if you're rotating offers later on, let's say you're testing a bunch of sweeps offers for Samsung Galaxy S9 in South Africa then [Any Other Info You Want] could be "Samsung Galaxy S9 Sweeps".

    Let's say I was promoting Gameloft for ZA Vodacom, example of an entire campaign name could be:

    110218 ZA Main Vodacom Gameloft
    -Again, leave "Group" blank.

    -Specify "Traffic Source" as "PropellerAds".

    -For "CPC/CPM/CPA", checkmark "Auto". (This way, the traffic costs shown in Binom will be based on what the traffic source sends back via tracking tokens - not all traffic sources can do that, but PropellerAds and PopAds, the two sources we'll be using in this tutorial - will both do this. So choosing "Auto" for both of these sources will be fine. For sources that can't send traffic cost data via tokens, you will need to click on either "CPC" or "CPM" here - most likely CPM if you're running pop - and then manually specify your bid.)

    -For "Hide referrer" leave at "None".

    -Set "Domain" to your tracker domain (if it isn't already).

    -Leave "Type of distribution" at "Normal rotation".

    -Ignore the "Campaign URL" for now - it's empty now but will be filled-in when you save the campaign later.

    -On the right-hand side of the screen, under "LANDING PAGES", click on "+ Direct", since we'll be linking directly to the offer (thus not using landing pages - something we'll cover in the future lesson).

    -Under "OFFERS" click on "+ Offer" and select the offer you're wanting to run.

    -Click the "Save" button at the top.

    -A "Campaign URL" will appear. Let's ignore that for now, but in tomorrow's lesson we'll be copying this campaign url and pasting it into PropellerAds to set up a campaign there to obtain traffic (i.e. buy visitors).





    We've been doing everything in step-by-step fashion up until now. Now that we've built some momentum, I'll start to remove the training wheels bit by bit - because ultimately if you're wanting to be a successful affiliate marketer, you'll need to learn to think on your own!

    So I'm going to ask you to figure out how to set up offers and create Binom campaigns for the other 2 affiliate networks in a similar way as for Mobidea above.

    I WILL, however, point out the differences. Let's go!


    1)Add "Clickdealer" to Binom as a new affiliate network.


    2)Copy the Binom postback url, log into Clickdealer, go to "Tools" > "Global postback". Paste it into "Global S2S postback URL", and replace tokens so the final postback will be in this format:

    https://yourdomain.com/click.php?cnv...payout=#price#
    Click "Save".

    (Note: This is the ONLY TIME you'll need to insert the postback into Clickdealer! The postback will work for all Clickdealer offers you run until you switch to another tracker.)


    3)Go to the Offers Spreadsheet and compile the "Final Offer Link"as follows.

    Original link format: http://cd-mob.com/?a=11111&c=130074

    Final link format: http://cd-mob.com/?a=11111&c=130074&s2={clickid}&s3={trafficsource}& s4={campaign}
    Again: Please make sure there are NO SPACES in the link!


    4)Add this offer into Binom as a new offer.


    5)Create a new campaign in Binom for this offer.


    There! Wasn't so bad, right? Let's do the same for the Gotzha offer next.





    1)Add "Gotzha" to Binom as a new affiliate network.


    2)Copy the Binom postback url, log into Gotzha, find the offer you've been approved for and click into offer details. Go to the "Testing & Tracking" tab, paste the postback into the "Postback URL" field, and replace tokens so the final postback will be in this format:

    http://track.yourdomain.com/click.ph...payout=#price#
    Click "Save".

    Note: For Mobidea and Clickdealer, you only needed to insert the postback url ONCE as "Global Postbacks" that applied to all offers on the network. For Gotzha however, the global postback can only be set by an AM - so please contact your AM by giving them this postback url to request for this to be done. What I've just advised for you to do for now, was specify the postback for JUST that ONE offer alone. Of course you can continue to do that for every Gotzha offer you run in the future, but a better option would be to ask your AM to set it as a global postback for you.


    3)Go to the Offers Spreadsheet and compile the "Final Offer Link"as follows.

    Original link format: http://trckr.global/?a=1111&c=8726&s1=

    Final link format: http://trckr.global/?a=1111&c=8726&s2={clickid}&s3={trafficsource}&s4= {campaign}
    Again: Please make sure there are NO SPACES in the link!


    4)Add this offer into Binom as a new offer.


    5)Create a new campaign in Binom for this offer.

    EXPLANATION

    Although the "ACTION" part of this lesson was straight-forward, to truly understand how tracking tokens work will require quite a bit of reading, thinking, and experience.

    Below, I will attempt an explanation. But don't get too worried if you don't understand all of it - when you are confused by tracking in the future (which I guarantee you will!), this would be the place to refer back to.


    So why do we need to use a tracker? The main uses of a tracker are as follows:


    1)Detect details about your visitors, and aggregate and display this data.

    Binom can detect details of each visitor, such as their IP, country, operating system, device/brand/model, carrier, connection type (3g vs. wifi), browser...etc. This will help you to optimize a campaign - e.g. if a particular OS is losing you money but the other OSs are turning a profit, then you would stop buying visitors from the unprofitable OS.

    You will learn how to optimize a campaign in later lessons.


    2)Control the flow of your traffic, i.e. where to send which visitor.

    When creating a campaign in Binom, you can specify where you want to send your visitors. For example you could send them directly to the offer page. Or send them to a landing page (aka lander, pre-lander, presell page, or bridge page) to presell them first, and when they click on a button on the landing page, send them to the offer page to increase the chances that they would subscribe to the offer.

    Without knowing all this data about your visitors and conversions, you'd be flying blind, as it would make it very difficult to optimize your campaigns.

    You could also specify multiple offers and/or landing pages to split-test them, to find out which one is the best, and then pause the inferior ones. You could also set up Binom Rules to redirect visitors, aka "traffic", based on certain conditions. For example, you can set up Binom Rules to redirect all Android traffic to one offer and all IOS traffic to another other, and the remaining traffic to a third offer.

    You will learn about landing pages and tracker rules in detail in later lessons. For now, just know that using a tracker is almost mandatory if you want to make money in this industry, which is why I've asked you to get used to using a tracker from the start.


    *****

    Next, I would like to explain some of the steps we've taken to set up tracking.

    The gist of the setup is as follows - using the Clickdealer offer as an example):

    -We grabbed the offer link from Clickdealer and put that into Binom.

    -We created a campaign in Binom, and specified direct-linking to the offer.

    -We took the postback url from Binom and put it into Clickdealer as the global postback.

    -In the next lesson we'll take the campaign url and paste it into the traffic network (PropellerAds).

    So this is what will happen:

    -PropellerAds will send visitors to the Binom campaign url.

    -Binom will then take over, and send visitors to the offer we specified in campaign settings.

    -If a visitor ends up subscribing to the offer, aka "converting", Clickdealer will record this, and "tell" Binom about the conversion, by triggering the postback url to send that conversion data to Binom. Binom will then display the conversion in its stats.

    As for how we modified the offer link from Original to Final: Remember that Binom will send visitors to the Final Offer Link you added to Binom (when you were adding "+ New Offer"). Let's say the Final Offer Link was this:

    http://cd-mob.com/?a=11111&c=130074&...&s4={campaign}

    {campaign}, {trafficsource}, and {clickid} are Binom tokens. When Binom triggers this link to send the visitor to the offer page, it will automatically replace these with actual values. And because this is a Clickdealer offer link, Clickdealer will read these values into its database and store them.

    So to clarify: Binom will trigger the offer link as follows for example (replacing the tokens with values):

    http://cd-mob.com/?a=11111&c=130074&...e&s3=10&s4=151

    And Clickdealer will store this visitor's data as a new row in its stats database. Imagine a spreadsheet with columns that say "s2", "s3", and "s4". Each visit will be logged in a new row. For this particular example, the value "12345abcde" would be logged for variable s2, the value "10" would be logged for variable s3, and the value "151" for variable s4.

    {campaign} is a random string of characters created by Binom to represent the new Binom campaign you've just created.
    {trafficsource} is a random string of characters created by Binom to represent the traffic source you've specified for this campaign.
    {clickid} is a random string of characters created by Binom to represent the visitor - it's almost like a name they assign to the visitor.

    As for how we set up the postback on Clickdealer: Remember we got the Postback URL from Binom, pasted that into Clickdealer and modified it to:

    http://track.yourdomain.com/postback...payout=#price#

    Whenever a conversion happens, Clickdealer will trigger this url to "tell" Binom that a conversion has been made. As it does so, Clickdealer will automatically insert the 2 values into the link - for example:

    https://yourdomain.com/click.php?cnv...de&payout=0.66

    The "12345abcde" comes from the database, where the {clickid} was stored. The payout of the offer, Clickdealer would of course know as well.

    So, effectively, by triggering this postback url, Clickdealer would be telling Binom that "hey! Visitor 12345abcde has converted with a payout of 0.66!"

    And Binom will then add this conversion to its stats.


    *****

    So why did we want to send {campaign} and {trafficsource} to Clickdealer's database?

    After visitors convert, the owners of the offers will try to monetize them to make back the commissions they paid to the affiliate network for each lead.

    The offer owner won't be able to make back the money they paid for every single lead. Let's take gaming subscription offers for example:

    Imagine if we were using landing pages to presell the visitor before sending them to the offer. If on the landing page we lie by telling the visitors that the game is free to play forever, then the conversion rate would be higher.

    However, after the user subscribes to the offer, they may be enraged to discover on their next phone bill, that the game isn't free after all. What do they do? They cancel - likely before the offer owner has made back their investment (i.e. the commissions they've paid to the affiliate network - which is also where our commissions would come from) .

    This type of leads will yield low life-time-value for the offer owner, and are therefore referred to leads with "bad lead quality". Whereas leads that will provide high life-time-value, have "good lead quality".

    And being misleading while pre-selling is only one of the factors that can influence lead quality. The visitors you buy from certain traffic sources can result in better or worse lead quality than other traffic sources. For example, leads from FB traffic will usually be of better quality than leads from pop traffic. In turn, not all pop sources have the same traffic quality.

    So why do we need to care about lead quality? Why not leave that for the offer owner to worry about?

    Answer: Offer owners are not stupid. If they can't make their money back from the leads we send them, they're not going to keep paying for leads from us.

    They would ask the affiliate network to tell us to stop promoting the offer.

    This is where sending the Campaign ID and Traffic Source ID to the affiliate network may potentially help us. This information gives the affiliate network and the offer owner the option of telling us which specific campaigns and/or traffic sources are responsible for the bad-quality leads, so that we'd have the option of stopping those campaigns or stop buying from individual traffic sources, and continue running the remaining campaigns. If we don't send the Campaign ID or Traffic Source ID, then when our lead quality is deemed unacceptable, the only solution would be for us to stop promoting the offer altogether, i.e. get kicked from the offer.

    It depends on the network.

    This -> {offer_url} is always used for the offer URL, the following parameters can change from network to network.

    Best is when you check what parameters your CPA network uses for click ID and then you add them to the template.

    For postback its similar, there the tokens can also change from network to network based on the parameters they use for the offer URLs.
