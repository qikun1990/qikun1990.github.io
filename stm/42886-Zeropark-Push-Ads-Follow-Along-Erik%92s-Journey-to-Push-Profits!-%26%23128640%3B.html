    WHAT ARE PUSH NOTIFICATIONS & PUSH TRAFFIC?

    I though most people understand what push traffic is, but when I met my friend earlier this month at AWA in Bangkok, to my surprise he didn‚Äôt know what push traffic really is..

    ..so let‚Äôs make it clear for now and forever:

    Do you own a smartphone?

    Good! Did you ever get a notification on your lock screen that you have a missed call?

    Or perhaps that your affiliate payout just arrived to your bank account?

    Or that your friend just replied to your post on Facebook?

    Well, these are all push notifications (on mobile).

    Every app is able to send you some kind of notifications including browsers such as Google Chrome.

    And with browsers it‚Äôs getting really interesting!

    WHY?

    The fact is that EVERY website with a special installed code is ABLE to SEND you a PUSH NOTIFICATION.

    I bet you've already seen these small popups in the browser address bar that asked you to allow notifications from this website.



    Once you subscribe, website owners are able to send you these push notifications.

    It's kinda similar to an email list except there is no email.

    These notifications work both on MOBILE and DESKTOP.

    There are companies/traffic sources that use this fact and are collecting huge numbers of push subscribers around the world.

    BOOM! This is where push traffic is coming from!

    TLDR; Push notification traffic is a traffic from push notifications that originates from browsers.

    The majority of the traffic is coming from mobile Android phones and tablets (no iOS traffic yet, but there is desktop MacOS traffic) and also Desktop.

    Otherwise the fact is, that I have solid experience with pop, redirect, adult display and a bit of native traffic.

    I would say that push notifications are something between pops and native.

    They are not as intrusive as pops, it‚Äôs rather on the user to decide if he clicks on the notification and view what‚Äôs following next.

    Icon

    SSL/HTTPS

    To run push ads on Zeropark your tracking domain needs to have SSL enabled, otherwise they won't accept your campaign.



    More info: https://zeropark.com/docs/zeropark-f...tps-campaigns/

    Honestly I didn't use SSL (https) yet as it was just working fine with good old http, but this finally pushed me to proceed.

    So I went ahead and setup my custom tracking domain on SSL.

    With the help of Voluum's support it was quick and easy to do.
